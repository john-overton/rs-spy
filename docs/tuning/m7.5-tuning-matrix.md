# M7.5 — Tuning matrix: unlock trade frequency without abandoning the doctrine

Written 2026-07-04, after the M7 validation-study review. This is the campaign plan for
iterating the M5 backtest toward a trade count large enough that the 08 §3 studies mean
something, while keeping the method recognizably RS/RW. Results are tracked in
`docs/tuning/ledger.csv` (committed — one row per run, including failures) with per-run
artifacts under `reports/tuning/<run_id>/` (gitignored, same folder-per-run layout the M8
GUI plan intends to use).

Companion reading: `IMPLEMENTATION.md` ("M7: full validation study suite" + known
limitations #18, #23, #24, #25), and the M7 review findings summarized in §1 below.

---

## 1. What we know going in (evidence base)

From the M7 studies and the post-M7 code review:

1. **The dip-arm path (Path B) is structurally unreachable — confirmed by code logic, not
   just statistics.** `next_state_long` arms on `rrs_prev < 0 <= rrs_now`, but staying
   `QUALIFIED` requires `gate_pass` every bar, which includes `rolling_rrs_m5 >= 1.0` on the
   *same series*. The previous bar can't be both `>= 1.0` and `< 0`. The RRS branch can never
   fire at any threshold/window/universe; the LRSI branch (needs LRSI < 20 while above VWAP
   with RRS ≥ 1) is a nearly-empty intersection. Audit evidence: 0/128 symbols ever reached
   `DIP_ARMED` long in 5 years; 100% of real trades entered via the market-wide trigger
   bypass.
2. **Gate antagonism at short RRS windows.** `rrs_m5 >= 1` is easiest to satisfy via a sharp
   burst; `not_one_candle_wonder` vetoes exactly those bursts. At `window=6` they are nearly
   mutually exclusive (0 trades); at `window=18` both can be green for sustained stretches
   (10 trades). The M7 window finding is a mechanism, not a lucky calibration.
3. **The only working entry path needs a coincidence its own preconditions fight.** Trigger
   bypass needs a QUALIFIED symbol-bar (0.0145% of symbol-bars) on a trigger bar (1.6% of
   bars), then a 2-consecutive-bar bias hold — which is anti-correlated with *fresh*
   trendline-breach triggers (the trigger fires at the flip; the hold requires the flip to be
   2+ bars old). Ablation: disabling bias unlocked AMD, whose signal bar qualified but whose
   prior bar didn't.
4. **Stops are placed at 1.0 × ATR(M5,14) — inside single-bar noise.** STZ ($0.11 stop on a
   $237 stock) and PNC ($0.24 on $234) were stopped out *inside their own entry bar*. The one
   winner (ORCL) had an earnings-inflated ATR ~6–13× wider in relative terms. The spec's own
   stop (07 §3) anchors to the dip's swing low with 1.0×ATR as the floor and 1.5×ATR as the
   cap — the implementation kept only the floor (known limitation #18). Tight stops also
   collapse realized risk: the notional cap binds first, so trades risked $9–$125 instead of
   the intended $500.
5. **Walk-away: 747 qualification signals → 6 scored trades.** Qualification is not the
   bottleneck; everything after it is.

From the source documents (`documents/`):

6. **The doctrine core is 4 rules, not 9 gates** (`Keeping-it-Really-Simple.md`): trade with
   the market's direction (bias), right side of VWAP on M5, HA continuation ≥ 2 days on D1,
   right side of all major SMAs on D1. These four map to our `bias`, `vwap`, `ha_cont_d1`,
   `sma_stack` gates — **they stay untouched in this campaign**. The quantified extras
   (`rrs_m5 >= 1`, `rrs_d1 >= 1`, one-candle-wonder, score ≥ 50, headroom, volume_d1, the
   2-bar bias hold, every-bar re-qualification) are spec-added translations — that is where
   the "too strict across the board" concern actually lives, and where we loosen.
7. **The real-life dip workflow contradicts our state machine — by design of ours, not
   theirs** (`Don't-Overthink-This.md`, a Pete/OneOption piece): spot a strong stock
   (M5 RS/RW **> 0** — note: not ≥ 1 — and/or LRSI > 80), set an alert, *walk away*. The
   alert fires when RS/RW goes `< 0` then `> 0` (or LRSI `< 20` then `> 20`). Nothing
   requires the stock to stay "qualified" during the dip — **the dip below zero is the
   point**. Re-evaluation (market OK? stock still looks good?) happens *at the alert*, not
   continuously. "Don't buy breakouts, set alerts instead. Buy dips and take gains when the
   bounce stalls."
8. **The market-timing signal is the engine of entries in real life too.** The community's
   pros time entries off a SPY signal (Pete's 1OP cross/convergence; our analog is the
   trendline-breach trigger). That 100% of our trades came via the trigger bypass is
   *agreement* with real-life practice, not an anomaly. And per the user: the same people
   who say "don't trade SPY" (no RS edge vs. itself — `Trading-SPY-QQQ` doc) do in practice
   trade SPY off the 1OP convergence signal alone. That gives us a clean falsifiable
   experiment: trade SPY itself on our trigger with no RS gates (group D below) — it
   isolates the quality of the timing signal every stock trade also depends on.
9. **Exits/time-in-trade are where the method says value is lost**
   (`The-Great-Imbalance.md`: "almost all of your picks are winners — it is just a matter of
   WHEN"), consistent with our walk-away MFE (4.6R available) vs. realized (−0.28R mean).

## 2. The levers

Legend — **Status**: `knob` = settable on `BacktestConfigM5` today, zero code;
`code-S` = small code change (constant → knob, or localized logic); `code-M` = a real task
(state-machine or stop-logic variant). **Doctrine?** = whether the lever touches one of the
4 core rules (we avoid those).

### Group A — entry-path structure (the unlock levers)

| ID | Lever | Baseline | Recommended setting(s) | Status | Rationale |
|----|-------|----------|------------------------|--------|-----------|
| A1 | Dip-arm "alert model": once QUALIFIED, hold candidacy on **D1 gates + score only** (D1 gates are constant within a session anyway); let intraday gates (`rrs_m5`, `vwap`, one-candle-wonder) fail freely during the dip; re-check bias + VWAP **at the arm/entry bar** | evicted to IDLE the bar any gate fails | Candidacy persists for the rest of the session (variant a) or a K-bar grace window, K=24 (~2h) (variant b) | code-M | Evidence #1 + #7. This is the faithful translation of the source workflow; the current every-bar re-qualification is the deviation. Makes the RRS zero-cross reachable for the first time. |
| A2 | Dip-arm cross basis | RRS crossing 0 (unreachable today) | Keep zero-cross once A1 lands (source-faithful); LRSI 20-cross stays as OR-branch | — | #7: "< 0 and then > 0" is the source's literal alert. |
| A3 | Bias 2-bar hold at trigger-bypass entries | required always | On trigger bars, accept bucket-only (no 2-bar hold); hold still required for non-trigger entries | code-S | #3: the hold is anti-correlated with fresh triggers. Ablation's AMD case is the smoking gun. |

### Group B — gate strictness (the "too strict" levers; quantified spec-extras only)

| ID | Lever | Baseline | Recommended sweep | Status | Rationale |
|----|-------|----------|-------------------|--------|-----------|
| B1 | `rrs_m5_threshold_long` / `_short` | 1.0 / −1.0 | {1.0, 0.5, **0.0**} / mirrored | knob | #6/#7: the source qualifies on RS/RW **> 0**. Our ≥ 1 gate passes 6.3% of bars — tightest gate in the stack. |
| B2 | `rrs_m5_window` | 12 | {12, **18**, 24} | knob | M7's strongest numeric finding (3 → 10 trades at 18); 18 was the *edge* of the swept grid, so probe beyond it. |
| B3 | `rrs_d1_threshold_long` / `_short` | 1.0 / −1.0 | {1.0, **0.5**} / mirrored | knob | Second-tightest quantified gate (18.4% pass). D1 strength should be a tendency, not a wall — the doctrine's D1 checks are HA + SMA, not an RRS number. |
| B4 | `min_list_score` / `min_hold_score` | 50 / 40 | hold at 50/40 for now; revisit only if Rounds 1–3 stall | knob | Score is a soft composite; loosening it while also loosening gates confounds attribution. One group per round. |

Deliberately **not** swept: `vwap`, `sma_stack`, `ha_cont_d1`, bias direction (doctrine core,
#6); `not_one_candle_wonder` (B2 already defuses it mechanically — widening the window is
the principled fix, deleting the anti-pattern check is not).

### Group C — stops & exits

| ID | Lever | Baseline | Recommended sweep | Status | Rationale |
|----|-------|----------|-------------------|--------|-----------|
| C1 | Stop distance multiplier (`STOP_ATR_MULT`, currently a constant in `algo/risk.py`) | 1.0 × ATR(M5) | {1.0, **1.5**, 2.0} × ATR(M5), promoted to a `BacktestConfigM5` knob | code-S | #4: two of three real trades died inside their entry bar to sub-0.1% stops. 1.5 is the spec's own cap — i.e., still spec-legal. |
| C2 | Swing-low-anchored stop (07 §3 faithful): stop = lower of (qualifying dip's swing low, entry − 1.0×ATR), skip entry if > 1.5×ATR away | ATR-only floor | on/off variant vs. best C1 cell | code-M | Known limitation #18. Structure-anchored stops are what the spec actually says; also restores the "skip untradeable setups" branch. |
| C3 | Same-bar stop evaluation (entry fill and stop check in one bar) | allowed | measure first (ledger column `same_bar_stop_rate`), only change if C1/C2 don't fix it | — | May be a conservative-but-fair fill convention; don't touch until the stop width itself is right. |

### Group D — market-timing layer (studies + one experiment; cheap, no confluence involved)

| ID | Lever / study | What it is | Status | Rationale |
|----|---------------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| D1 | Trigger forward-return study | Confusion-matrix / forward-R distribution of SPY returns after `LONG_TRIGGER` / `SHORT_TRIGGER` fires (1,591 / 561 fires — real sample sizes!), horizons {6, 12, 24} bars | code-S (study script only, no backtest) | The M7 confusion matrix tested the *bucket* (no skill found); the signal that actually gates 100% of entries is the *trigger*, never yet tested. | D2 | SPY direct-trade experiment | Trade SPY itself on trigger fires: M5 stops per C-group settings, no RS gates (RS-vs-itself is undefined), long+short | code-M | #8: the 1option pros do this in practice off 1OP convergence. ## 3. Phase plan — one group per round, promote winners to baseline

**Iteration protocol**: every run gets a `run_id` (`r<round><cell>`, e.g. `r2b1w18t05`), a
row in `ledger.csv`, and a folder `reports/tuning/<run_id>/` containing `config.json`,
`trades.csv`, and (once D3 exists) `funnel.json`. The baseline config is only updated
("promoted") per the decision rules in §4, and every promotion is a ledger row with
`verdict=PROMOTED`.

### Phase 0 — enablers (code, no tuning yet)
- **0a** Promote `STOP_ATR_MULT` to a `BacktestConfigM5` knob (C1 prerequisite). Trivial.
- **0b** Build D3 funnel instrumentation into `run_m5_backtest` (emit counters; write
  `funnel.json` from the CLI). Small.
- **0c** Fix known limitation #23 (dip-arm `prev` read on the reindexed frame → NaN on gappy
  symbols silently suppresses crossings). Must land *before* A1, or the alert model is
  handicapped on thin names — the exact names a future universe expansion adds.
- **0d** Known limitation #24: let `run_m5_backtest` accept a pre-built `PreparedM5`, and/or
  split feature precompute from gate evaluation. Today every cell pays the full 15–20 min
  precompute even for threshold-only changes (gates/scores are baked in at prepare time).
  With ~20 cells planned, this pays for itself in the first round. Strongly recommended
  before Round 2.
- **0e** D1 trigger forward-return study script (no backtest loop needed — pure series math
  over the existing bias/trigger output). Run it immediately; its result shapes how much
  weight Round 4 gets.

### Round 1 — structural unlock (Group A)
Everything else at baseline. Cells:
- `r1a`: A1 variant a (session-persistent candidacy) + A2.
- `r1b`: A1 variant b (K=24-bar grace) + A2.

Success = `DIP_ARMED > 0` and first-ever Path B trades. Compare a/b on trade count *and*
trade quality; pick one variant, promote.

### Round 2 — strictness sweep (Group B), on top of Round 1's winner
3×3 grid, 9 cells: B1 `rrs_m5_threshold` ∈ {1.0, 0.5, 0.0} × B2 `window` ∈ {12, 18, 24}.
Then a 2-cell B3 follow-up ({1.0, 0.5}) on the grid winner. All knobs exist today — this
round is pure runtime (hence 0d).

Watch for: trade count should rise smoothly toward looser/wider; if a cell spikes
non-monotonically, suspect noise, don't promote it.

### Round 3 — stops (Group C), on top of Round 2's winner
- `r3-c1-{10,15,20}`: C1 multiplier sweep.
- `r3-c2`: C2 swing-low variant vs. the best C1 cell.

Primary metrics here are *quality*: same-bar-stop rate, hard-stop share of exits, avg R,
expectancy — not trade count (stops don't create entries).

### Round 4 — market layer (Groups A3 + D), on top of Round 3's winner
- `r4-a3`: bias-hold-at-trigger relaxation on/off (D3's funnel from earlier rounds will
  already have measured how many entries the hold killed — this round confirms causally).
- `r4-d2`: the SPY direct-trade experiment (parallel track, doesn't touch the stock system).

### Round 5 — only if needed
Universe expansion (M3.5 precedent: linear multiplier, but fix 0c first), `min_list_score`,
headroom/volume gates. Re-run the full M7 study suite on the promoted baseline once trade
count ≥ 30 — that's the point where ablation/time-of-day/walk-away become readable.

## 4. Decision rules (overfitting guardrails)

1. **One group per round.** Never promote two groups' changes from the same comparison.
2. **Promote only if**: trade count materially up (or, for Group C, quality up at equal
   count) **and** expectancy/profit factor not collapsed **and** the effect is consistent
   across adjacent cells (the M3.5 standard: direction agreement across the grid, not one
   hot cell).
3. **Milestones, not targets**: ≥ 30 trades → studies become directionally readable;
   ≥ 100 → statistics start to mean something. The spec's aspiration ("5 good trades a day")
   is ~6,000 trades/5yr — nobody expects a round to get there; don't chase it.
4. **Every run goes in the ledger, including duds.** A cell that produced 0 trades is a
   finding (window=6 was one of M7's most mechanistically informative results).
5. **Doctrine gates stay** (bias direction, VWAP side, HA ≥ 2, SMA stack). If a round only
   "works" by gutting one of those, the campaign has left RS/RW and the result doesn't count.
6. Sample sizes stay small in absolute terms all campaign — every conclusion is flagged
   directional until the ≥ 100 milestone, matching M3.5/M7's own reporting practice.

## 5. Ledger schema (`docs/tuning/ledger.csv`)

One row per run. `config_delta` is a compact JSON of only the fields changed from the
*current promoted baseline* at the time of the run; `baseline_ref` names the baseline row it
was run against, so the chain of promotions is reconstructible.

| column | meaning |
|--------|---------|
| run_id | `r<round><cell>` slug, also the `reports/tuning/` folder name |
| date | run date |
| round | 0–5 |
| baseline_ref | run_id of the promoted baseline this run was based on |
| code_ref | git commit the run was executed at |
| config_delta | JSON of changed fields (empty `{}` for baseline reruns) |
| n_trades / n_long / n_short | trade counts |
| win_rate, profit_factor, avg_r, expectancy, max_dd_pct, trades_per_day, total_pnl | 08 §2 metrics |
| same_bar_stop_rate | fraction of trades with entry_time == exit_time |
| exit_mix | JSON: counts by exit_reason |
| n_qualified, n_dip_armed, n_path_b_trades, n_trigger_coincidences | funnel counters (blank until 0b lands) |
| verdict | `PROMOTED` / `REJECTED` / `INFO` |
| notes | one line: what this run was asking, what it answered |

The M7 baseline (3 trades) is pre-seeded as row 1 so every future run has an anchor.
